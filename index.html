About

This website is dedicated to the Mr. Olympia bodybuilding competition, specifically focusing on the three main divisions: Open, Classic Physique, and 212 Bodybuilding. Each division has its own unique criteria and judging standards, allowing athletes to showcase their physiques in different ways. The Open division is known for its sheer size and muscularity, while Classic Physique emphasizes aesthetics and symmetry. The 212 division caters to athletes who weigh 212 pounds or less, showcasing their conditioning and muscle definition. This site aims to provide information about these divisions, their history, and the athletes who compete in them.

What Is Mr. Olympia?

Mr. Olympia is the most prestigious bodybuilding competition in the world, held annually as part of the Joe Weider’s Olympia Fitness & Performance Weekend. This event brings together the top bodybuilders who have qualified through professional competitions to compete for the coveted title of Mr. Olympia and other divisional championships. Winning Mr. Olympia is the ultimate achievement in professional bodybuilding, symbolizing peak muscular development, conditioning, and symmetry.

What Is Bodybuilding?

Bodybuilding is a sport focused on developing and sculpting the human physique through resistance training, diet, and discipline. Unlike powerlifting or weightlifting, which focus on strength, bodybuilding emphasizes muscle size, definition, symmetry, and aesthetics. Competitors showcase their physiques through mandatory poses and routines, judged on muscle mass, proportion, symmetry, and conditioning.

When Does Mr. Olympia Take Place?

It typically happens in November or December with:

  • Prejudging rounds
  • Finals & posing routines
  • Expo events

How Many Competitors Are in Each Division?

The number of athletes in each division varies, as competitors must qualify by winning or placing high in IFBB Pro League contests. On average:

  • Men’s Open Bodybuilding – 15–20 competitors
  • Classic Physique – 25–30 competitors
  • 212 Bodybuilding – 15–20 competitors

Each category has its own rules and judging criteria, with competitors showcasing their strengths in their respective divisions. There are a lot more divisions, including the ones on the female side, but in this website, we will only cover Open, Classic, and 212.
